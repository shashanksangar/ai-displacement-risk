# ⚠️ Important Disclaimer

## About This Project

This is an **educational and exploratory tool** for understanding potential AI impacts on different occupations. It should **not** be used as:
- Professional career advice
- A definitive prediction of job displacement
- A guarantee of job security

## About the Data

### Data Source
The occupation data comes from the **U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Outlook Handbook**, which provides authoritative information about 342 occupations including:
- Job descriptions
- Required education and training
- Employment statistics
- Category classifications

### AI Exposure Scores
The AI exposure ratings (0-10 scale) are **subjective assessments** that were originally created as an exploratory visualization project. These scores:

- ✅ **Are**: Thought-provoking starting points for discussion
- ✅ **Are**: Based on general characteristics of digital vs. physical work
- ✅ **Are**: Useful for exploring automation concepts

- ❌ **Are NOT**: Scientific predictions
- ❌ **Are NOT**: Professional career assessments
- ❌ **Are NOT**: Guarantees of job security or displacement

### Original Project Note
This project was inspired by a visualization created by Andrej Karpathy that explored AI exposure across occupations using BLS data. That original project was taken down because it was "wildly misinterpreted" despite clear documentation that it was an exploratory weekend project.

**We've learned from that and want to be crystal clear**: These scores are **exploratory and subjective**, not scientific predictions.

## How to Use This Tool Responsibly

### ✅ Good Uses:
- **Education**: Understanding general concepts about AI and automation
- **Exploration**: Discovering how different jobs might be affected by AI
- **Discussion**: Starting conversations about workforce development
- **Awareness**: Learning about AI skills and their potential value
- **Curiosity**: Exploring the BLS occupational data in an interactive way

### ❌ Inappropriate Uses:
- **Career Decisions**: Don't base major career choices solely on these scores
- **Hiring/Firing**: Don't use this to make employment decisions
- **Investment**: Don't use this for financial or business planning
- **Fearmongering**: Don't use this to scare people about AI
- **Definitive Claims**: Don't present these scores as scientific fact

## The Reality of AI Impact

The actual impact of AI on employment is:
- **Complex**: Depends on regulation, adoption rates, economic factors, social acceptance
- **Uncertain**: Technology evolves in unpredictable ways
- **Nuanced**: AI often augments rather than replaces workers
- **Context-Dependent**: Industry, company, and individual factors matter
- **Long-Term**: Changes happen over years/decades, not overnight

## About AI Skills

While this tool shows how AI skills might reduce displacement risk, remember:
- Different jobs benefit from different skills
- Skills are just one factor in career success
- Continuous learning matters more than any single skill
- Human skills (creativity, empathy, judgment) remain crucial
- Technology is a tool to augment human capability

## Limitations

This tool has several important limitations:

1. **Subjective Scoring**: AI exposure scores are opinions, not measurements
2. **Static Data**: Doesn't account for rapid changes in AI technology
3. **Simplified Model**: Real-world career resilience is far more complex
4. **Missing Factors**: Doesn't consider regulation, unions, social factors, economic conditions
5. **General Categories**: Doesn't account for variation within occupations
6. **Skill Weighting**: Skill impact calculations are approximations, not exact science

## Our Intent

We created this tool to:
- Make BLS occupational data more accessible and interactive
- Encourage thinking about AI's role in different types of work
- Promote AI literacy and upskilling
- Provide a starting point for career conversations
- Demonstrate interactive data visualization

We **did not** create it to:
- Predict the future
- Replace professional career counseling
- Make definitive claims about job security
- Cause anxiety about automation
- Provide investment or business advice

## Recommended Actions

If you're concerned about AI's impact on your career:

1. **Seek Professional Advice**: Talk to career counselors, industry mentors, or workforce development specialists
2. **Do Your Own Research**: Look at multiple sources, academic studies, industry reports
3. **Focus on Continuous Learning**: Regardless of AI, staying current is always valuable
4. **Develop Human Skills**: Creativity, critical thinking, emotional intelligence, communication
5. **Stay Informed**: Follow reputable sources on AI development and labor market trends
6. **Be Adaptable**: The best preparation is being flexible and willing to learn
